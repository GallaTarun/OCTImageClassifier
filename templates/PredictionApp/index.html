Retinal Disease Diagnosis

This website is powered by DJANGO Framework. A Deep Learning model is trained on a dataset containing nearly 85,000 Optical Coherence Tomography(OCT) images of Retina. The trained model is VGG-16 which is considered one of the best among the existing Convolutional Neural Networks pre-defined in the KERAS-API. It is trained on IMAGENET dataset. Using Transfer Learning technique, the pre-trained weights are assigned to our current deep learning model. Now, the current model is capable of diagnosing the Retinal Disease given the OCT image of the retina.

The model is capable of predicting the given input OCT image into one of following categories.

  • CHOROIDAL NEOVASCULARIZATION
  • DIABETIC MACULAR EDEMA
  • DRUSEN
  • NORMAL

CNV Choroidal NeoVascularization is the formation of new blood vessels in the choroid layer of the eye. It is caused by the cracks within the macular tissue of the retina. It can cause sudden deterioriation of the central vision.
DME The blood vessels in the back of the eye swell, weaken and leak. This occurs in a part of the retina called the macula. Diabetic Macular Edema can cause too much fluid to build up in the macula, which can lead to vision changes if left untreated.
DRUSEN Drusen are small yellow deposits of fatty proteins(lipids) that accumulate under the retina. It also leads to damage in central vision of the eye.
NORMAL RETINA A normal and healthy retina with no issues.
